## Research Threads

### Thread 1: Space-Time Compression â€” From Geometry to Information

**Goal:** Build a reasoning pipeline that can trace the transformation of space-time geometry under extreme compression, connecting the smooth manifold picture of GR to the discrete/informational picture suggested by quantum gravity.

Key topics:
- The Schwarzschild and Kerr metrics â€” how curvature diverges as r approaches 0
- Penrose-Hawking singularity theorems â€” what "geodesic incompleteness" really means
- BKL (Belinsky-Khalatnikov-Lifshitz) dynamics â€” the chaotic, oscillatory approach to the singularity
- The Planck scale â€” where space-time itself may become discrete, foamy, or emergent
- Holographic principle â€” the radical idea that the information content of a volume scales with its *surface area*, not its volume
- The Bekenstein-Hawking entropy formula: S = A / (4 l_P^2)

### Thread 2: The Inner Life of Black Holes

**Goal:** Explore the question of what, if anything, constitutes the "interior experience" of a black hole â€” both in the physical sense (what an infalling observer would encounter) and in the information-theoretic sense (what computational or dynamical processes unfold inside).

Key topics:
- The experience of an infalling observer â€” tidal forces, spaghettification, and the surprisingly gentle crossing of a supermassive black hole's horizon
- The interior Schwarzschild geometry â€” the "river model" where space itself flows inward faster than light
- The Cauchy horizon and mass inflation instability in Kerr (rotating) black holes
- The black hole interior as a "longest road" â€” the maximal proper time to the singularity
- Black hole complementarity (Susskind) â€” the idea that interior and exterior descriptions are complementary, never contradictory, because no single observer accesses both
- The firewall paradox (AMPS) â€” does quantum mechanics demand a wall of high-energy radiation at the horizon?
- ER=EPR (Maldacena-Susskind) â€” the conjecture that entanglement *is* geometric connectivity; every pair of entangled particles is connected by a microscopic wormhole

### Thread 3: Information, Unitarity, and the Fate of Quantum States

**Goal:** Reason about the black hole information paradox â€” arguably the deepest unsolved problem at the intersection of quantum mechanics and gravity.

Key topics:
- Hawking radiation and the information paradox â€” how a black hole's thermal evaporation seems to destroy quantum information
- Page curves and the Page time â€” when the entanglement entropy of radiation should begin to decrease
- The island formula and quantum extremal surfaces â€” recent breakthroughs suggesting information *does* escape, via subtle quantum gravitational effects
- Replica wormholes and the Euclidean path integral â€” how summing over non-trivial topologies resolves the paradox
- Implications for unitarity â€” is quantum mechanics fundamentally exact, or does gravity violate it?

### Thread 4: Computational and Philosophical Implications

**Goal:** Connect the physics of black holes to broader questions about computation, complexity, and the nature of understanding.

Key topics:
- Black holes as the fastest scramblers in nature â€” the scrambling time scales as log(S)
- Quantum computational complexity and the interior â€” the "complexity = volume" and "complexity = action" conjectures
- The stretched horizon as a quantum computer â€” does the event horizon process information?
- The relationship between space-time geometry and quantum circuit complexity
- Philosophical questions: If the interior of a black hole is real but permanently inaccessible to outside observers, what does "real" mean? What does the black hole teach us about the limits of knowledge?
